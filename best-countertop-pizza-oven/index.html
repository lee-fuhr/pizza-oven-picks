Frequently asked questions

Can a countertop pizza oven beat a regular oven for pizza?

Yes, in most cases. A dedicated countertop pizza oven reaches higher temperatures than a standard kitchen oven and concentrates heat from both above and below, which is what produces a properly cooked crust and melted top at the same time. The result is closer to a pizzeria outcome than a standard oven on its highest setting, even with a pizza stone. The trade-off is counter space and a unit that does one thing very well rather than many things adequately.

What size pizza fits in a countertop oven?

Most countertop pizza ovens accommodate a 12-inch pizza, which is a standard medium. Some compact models are designed for smaller 10-inch or personal-size pies. Check the interior cooking surface dimensions before buying if you have a specific size in mind, and note that the interior may be smaller than the oven’s external footprint.

Are countertop pizza ovens good for other foods?

Yes. The same high heat and efficient top-and-bottom cooking that works for pizza also makes countertop ovens good for flatbreads, pita, naan, and other items that benefit from intense short cooking. Some models include temperature settings and accessories suited for bread, cookies, and other baked goods. Versatility varies by model.
