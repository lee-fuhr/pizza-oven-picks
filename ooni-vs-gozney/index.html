Ooni vs Gozney

Updated June 2026Independently researchedNo paid placement.

Quick answer

If you want maximum model variety, a strong beginner entry, and portability, go with Ooni. If you prefer a premium, heavy-duty oven that doubles as a backyard statement piece, choose Gozney. The real dealbreaker is whether you need a portable oven or a permanent setup.

What are the main differences in Ooni and Gozney’s lineup philosophy?

Ooni’s lineup is built around versatility and accessibility. They offer several models at different price points, from a very affordable entry-level oven to a pro-grade one with a built-in thermometer and larger cooking surface. This approach makes it easy for someone new to pizza ovens to find an option that fits their budget and space. Ooni also updates their line often, introducing models like the Koda and Fyra to cover gas, wood, and pellet cooking in compact packages. Gozney takes a more curated approach with fewer models, each designed as a long-term investment. Their flagship Dome is a heavy, powder-coated steel oven meant to live outdoors permanently. The Roccbox is slightly more portable but still heavier than most Oonis. Gozney’s philosophy is about build quality and aesthetics first, each oven feels like a piece of outdoor equipment you’ll keep for years. If you want a broad choice and the ability to start small, Ooni wins. If you want to buy once and buy something that looks stunning, Gozney is the play.

How do their designs and build quality compare?

Ooni ovens are engineered for portability. Most models have folding legs, a relatively light weight, and compact profiles that make them easy to carry to a campsite or store in a shed. The exterior is typically powder-coated steel or stainless steel, and the cooking floor is a thick stone. Ooni’s aesthetic is modern and minimal, functional without much ornamentation. Gozney’s Dome is the opposite: a chunky, retro-looking dome with a Cor-Ten steel or stainless steel finish that patinas over time. It’s significantly heavier and requires a dedicated cart or stone table. The Roccbox is also built like a tank, with a silicone band for handling hot surfaces. Neither Gozney model truly folds or packs flat. If you have the space and want your oven to be a visual centerpiece, Gozney’s design is hard to beat. If you need something you can move around, Ooni’s lighter, folding design is much more practical.

Which fuel options does each brand offer?

Ooni offers a fuel choice for nearly every preference. The Koda runs on propane gas for convenience, the Fyra uses wood pellets for an authentic flavor, and the Karu is a multi-fuel oven that accepts wood, charcoal, or an optional gas burner. This level of flexibility means you can switch between fuels on the same oven with an accessory, or buy a dedicated model for your primary fuel. Ooni also sells branded gas regulators and hose kits. Gozney’s fuel options are simpler. The Roccbox is gas-only unless you buy a separate wood-pellet attachment, which many owners find fiddly. The Dome comes in either a dedicated gas version or a dedicated wood-fired version (with a gas option for the wood model via an accessory burner). Gozney’s gas hookups require a fixed natural gas line or propane tank, and the wood-fired Dome uses split logs or large chunks. If you value fuel flexibility across one brand, Ooni gives you more choices. If you want a straightforward gas or wood experience with minimal accessories, Gozney’s approach is cleaner.

How do temperature performance and real-world cooking compare?

Both Ooni and Gozney claim peak temperatures well over 900°F, and in practice, both get hot enough for Neapolitan-style pizza in under 60 seconds. Ooni’s thin stone floors heat up quickly, many users report hitting cooking temps in under 20 minutes, but they can lose heat faster when you open the door frequently. Gozney’s thicker floor and heavier insulation in the Dome retain heat noticeably longer, making back-to-back pizzas more consistent. The Roccbox also holds heat well thanks to its insulated walls. In real-world cooking, Ooni ovens tend to have a hotter ceiling flame, which can char the top of the pizza quickly if you aren’t watching. Gozney’s dome design distributes heat more evenly around the pie, reducing the need for constant turning. That said, both produce excellent results. The main difference is how forgiving the ovens are: Gozney offers a slightly wider sweet spot, while Ooni rewards attentive baking.

How do the accessory ecosystems compare?

Ooni has a huge accessory catalog. You can buy a dedicated turning peel, an infrared thermometer, a pizza cuter, gas conversion kits, pellet hopper covers, and even branded carrying bags. The ecosystem is designed to make your cooking easier from day one, and everything integrates cleanly with their ovens. Many third-party accessories also fit Ooni models because of their standardized dimensions. Gozney’s accessory line is smaller but more targeted. They sell a very good turning peel, a Dome-specific door with a thermometer, a natural gas conversion kit, and a cart for the Dome. The accessories feel premium and match the oven’s styling. However, you won’t find a Gozney-branded pellet adapter for the Roccbox (it’s a separate attachment you buy elsewhere). If you like the idea of one brand for everything, Ooni’s ecosystem is more complete. If you prefer a curated set of high-quality accessories and don’t mind a smaller selection, Gozney delivers that.

Which brand is better for you? One question decides it.

The single most important question is: Do you need an oven that moves, or an oven that stays? If you plan to take your oven to tailgates, camping trips, or the beach, Ooni is the obvious choice. Its lightweight, folding-leg designs and available carrying cases make it genuinely portable. Ooni also starts at a very accessible price, which lowers the barrier to entry. If you have a permanent outdoor kitchen or a dedicated bar cart where the oven will live year-round, Gozney’s heavier build and superior heat retention pay off. The Dome especially becomes a statement piece that doesn’t need to be stored away. Gozney ovens also command a premium price, but owners often say the build quality justifies it. So ask yourself: portability or permanence? That one answer will point you to the right brand.

Frequently asked questions

Are Ooni and Gozney interchangeable in terms of pizza quality?

Yes, both can produce excellent Neapolitan-style pizzas with char and leopard spotting. The main difference is consistency: Gozney’s thicker insulation gives you a wider margin for error, while Ooni rewards careful technique.

Can you use wood pellets in a Gozney Roccbox?

You can buy a separate wood-pellet attachment for the Roccbox, but it’s not included and many users find it produces less heat than gas. The attachment works, but the Roccbox is primarily designed as a gas oven.

Which brand heats up faster?

Ooni ovens generally reach cooking temperature faster, often in 15–20 minutes, because of their thinner materials. Gozney ovens take a bit longer to heat up but hold that heat longer during multiple bakes.

Do both brands come with a gas attachment included?

Ooni includes a gas regulator in the box for models like the Koda and Karu 16. Gozney includes a gas regulator for the Roccbox and Dome gas models, but the Dome wood-fired version requires purchasing a separate gas burner accessory.

Which is easier to clean?

Ooni ovens are easier to clean because you can lift out the stone floor and brush off ash. Gozney’s Dome has a fixed stone that’s harder to remove, but the heavy steel interior can be scraped clean after the oven cools.

Is the Gozney Dome worth the higher price tag?

If you have the budget and a permanent outdoor spot, yes. The Dome’s build quality, heat retention, and aesthetic appeal justify its premium price. For a first pizza oven or a portable unit, Ooni offers better value.

In shortBoth Ooni and Gozney make excellent pizza ovens that can deliver restaurant-quality pies at home. Ooni gives you more models, more fuel options, and a price that fits almost any budget, plus true portability. Gozney focuses on fewer, heavier, premium builds that look great and perform consistently. Your choice comes down to whether you value versatility and mobility, or a permanent, statement-making oven that offers a slight edge in heat retention. Either way, you’ll be eating great pizza.
